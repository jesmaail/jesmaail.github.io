SOLID, DRY, and WET

Not the words themselves, but acronyms.

SOLID

Single-responsiblity principle (SRP)
> A class should have only a single responsiblity.
Open/closed principle (OCP)
> Software entities should be open for extension, closed for modification.
Liskov substitution principle (LSP)
> Objects in a program should be replaceable with instancesof their subtypes without altering the correctnessof the program.
Interface segregation principle (ISP)
> Many client-specfic interfaces are better than one general-purpose interface.
Dependency inversion principle (DIP)
> One should "depend upon abstractions", not upon concretions.

Single-responsibility principle - Expanded

  • Every module/class should have responsibility over a single part of functionality provided by the software.
  • That responsibility should be encapsulated by the class
"A CLASS SHOULD HAVE ONLY ONE REASON TO CHANGE"
Example: A module that compiles and prints reports.
  • Could be changed for 2 reasons
    1. Content of report could change (substantive).
    2. Format of report could change (cosmetic).
  • Both very different causes, therefore 2 different responsibilities.
  • These should be seperated into different classes/modules.

DRY

> Don't Repeat Yourself
  • Principle of software development aimed at reducing repetition of information of all kinds.
  • Every piece of knowledge must have a single, unambiguous, authoritative representation in the system.
  • When applied successfully -> modification of a single element doesn't require a change in other logically unrelated elements.

  • Violations of DRY are known as WET.
